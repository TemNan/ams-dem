In case of arrest, there is an arrestee support Group (AG) that helps you.

The arrestee support group can be reached on the number: 06 106 576 55

Contact the AG (preferably via Signal) if:

You see someone someone getting arrested

Your acquaintance/friend/buddy has been arrested

You took a photo/video of an arrest

The following information is important for us to know:

  1. Description of the arrested person
  2. Time and place of the arrest
  3. If you know the person:
  1. If this person uses medication and if yes what kind(s)
  2. If this person has outstanding fines or penalties
  3. If this person has their own lawyer, and if yes who
  4. Does someone need to be told about the situation? If yes, what are their contact details?
  5. What should this person be told?

You can also contact the AG if you are afraid someone has been arrested.

Always let the AG know if someone has been released!!

What to do when you see someone getting arrested:  

  1. Ask the person to yell out their (nick)name
  2. Take photos or videos of the arrest (but do not share the images online!!)

What to do/know if you get arrested yourself:  

  1. NEVER DECLARE ANYTHING ABOUT YOURSELF OR OTHERS!! You can ask why you’ve been arrested, ask for your lawyer and don’t say anything else (except maybe ‘no comment’).
  2. You have the right to see your own lawyer, but you will have to ask for them yourself! Our lawyer is JAANTJE KRAMER from Jebbink & Soeteman. If you see a lawyer, always ask to see their card! Before the police can interrogate you, you have the right to see your lawyer first. From the moment you have asked for your lawyer and they have been notified about this, they have 2 hours to come visit you. DO NOT accept a ‘piket’ lawyer (pro deo) or excuses from the police that they cannot arrange for you to see your own lawyer, or that it will cost money.
  3. When you are released, please contact the AG. They want to know how many people have been arrested and when you have been released. They can help you with advise on what to do.
  4. The police will ask you to sign a paper to get your stuff back. DO NOT SIGN ANYTHING.
  5. You have the right to vegan/vegetarian/halal/kosher food, and if necessary medical attention.
  6. Women can only be frisked by female agents, men only by male agents. For queer and trans*persons they will decide which sex they think you have (or what is in your passport).
  7. You have the right to see an interpreter during interrogation.
